No one enters a relationship expecting it to end, however planning for the future can offer stability and peace of mind. A Financial Agreement is a legally recognised way for couples, whether married, de facto, or in same-sex relationships, to set out how their assets, liabilities, and financial resources will be managed if the relationship breaks down.

These agreements are governed by the Family Law Act 1975 and must meet strict legal requirements to be valid and binding. To be binding, each party must:

* Sign the agreement;

* Receive independent legal advice about its effect on their rights; and

* Understand the advantages and disadvantages of entering into the agreement.

A Financial Agreement can be made at any stage, before, during, or after a relationship. In addition to outlining how property and financial resources will be divided, a Financial Agreement can also address issues such as maintenance or ongoing support for adult children.
